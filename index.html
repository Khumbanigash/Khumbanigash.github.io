2 Health and Supplements

2.1 Omega 3-6-9 Supplement

Which foods contain Omega 3 fats

  • Salmon
  • Mackerel
  • Sardines
  • Anchovies
  • Chia Seeds
  • Walnuts
  • Flaxseeds

Which foods contain Omega -6 fats

  • Soybean oil
  • Corn oil
  • Mayonnaise
  • Walnuts
  • Sunflower seeds
  • Almonds
  • Cashew nuts

Which foods contain Omega -9 fats

  • Vegetable and seed oils
  • Nuts
  • Seeds

How to choose an omega 3-6-9 supplement

Combined omega-3-6-9 supplements provide optimal ratios of fatty acids. However, they likely provide no additional benefits compared with omega-3 supplements.

  • Choose one that’s cold pressed. This means the oil has been extracted with limited heat, minimizing the oxidization that can damage the fatty acid molecules.
  • To ensure you’re taking a supplement that isn’t oxidized, choose one that contains an antioxidant such as vitamin E.
  • Additionally, select a supplement with the highest omega-3 content — ideally more than 0.3 grams per serving.
  • Since EPA and DHA have more health benefits than ALA. Choose a supplement that uses fish oil or algal oil, rather than flaxseed oil.

The bottom line

Combined omega-3-6-9 supplements are popular, but they generally provide no additional benefit over taking omega-3 alone.

Omega-6s are essential in certain quantities, but they’re present in many foods. People who follow a Western diet may already consume too many.

Additionally, the body can produce omega-9 fats, and they’re easily obtained in the diet. So you don’t need to take them in supplement form.

Therefore, although combined supplements contain optimal omega 3-6-9 ratios, taking just omega-3s will likely provide you with the most health benefits.

4 Recipes

4.1 Sogi

  1. Melt 200g butter in a pot with 2-3 cinnamon sticks and 1/4 teaspoon elaichi. Add 1 cup of semolina after butter melts and stir until cooked and combined properly.
  2. Then add 1 cup of boiling water. Mix until lumps are gone and moisture has simmered down. Approximately 2 min.
  3. Then add 1 cup of milk and 5ml(1tsp) vanilla essence. Keep stiring until combined and moisture simmers down and it begins to resemble soji texture.It should be smooth and soft...not lumpy
  4. Now you can add 1 cup of sugar or 1 can of condensed milk. I prefer the condensed milk. Keep stiring until moisture simmers and sohi texture starts to take form again.
  5. It will be watery everytime liquids are added, but you have to keep mixing until everything gets combined and takes soji form.
  6. Finally add 1/4 milk and almonds and mix until you get the texture you want.Then top with desert cream and sprinkle leftover almonds.
